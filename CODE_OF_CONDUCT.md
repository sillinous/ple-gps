# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Post-Labor Economics & Global Progress community is committed to being a space where people with diverse backgrounds, expertise levels, and perspectives can engage seriously with difficult ideas — without harassment, gatekeeping, or bad-faith behavior.

We prioritize the **quality of discourse** and the **dignity of participants** in equal measure.

---

## Standards

**We expect:**

- Engaging with ideas on their merits
- Disagreeing without dismissing
- Acknowledging uncertainty and complexity
- Crediting others' contributions
- Welcoming questions from those newer to the topic
- Flagging problems directly rather than complaining elsewhere

**We don't tolerate:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Discrimination based on race, gender, sexuality, disability, nationality, age, religion, or economic status
- Bad-faith arguments designed to exhaust rather than engage
- Misrepresenting others' positions
- Doxing or sharing private information
- Sustained off-topic disruption

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces: GitHub issues, discussions, pull requests, and any events or communications conducted under the PLE/GPS banner.

---

## Enforcement

Reports go to: **conduct@foundationforglobalprogress.org**

All reports are reviewed by the stewardship team. Responses may include: clarification request, warning, temporary suspension, or permanent removal — depending on severity and pattern.

We commit to responding within 7 days and to explaining our reasoning when enforcement action is taken.
