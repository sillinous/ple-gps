# PCF Category 1.0 — Develop Vision and Strategy

**Domain:** Operating Process | **Value Type:** Directional
**Feeds Into:** All operating categories (2–6), 9.1, 13.4
**Receives From:** 3.1, 6.4, 11.1, 12.1, 12.2, 13.7

---

## L2: Process Groups

### 1.1 Define the Business Concept and Long-Term Vision

**Purpose:** Establish the foundational "why" — what the organization exists to do,
who it serves, and what it aspires to become over a 5–20 year horizon.

#### L3 Processes
```
1.1.1  Assess the external environment
1.1.2  Survey the competitive landscape
1.1.3  Identify and analyze stakeholder needs/expectations
1.1.4  Evaluate the existing organizational capabilities
1.1.5  Establish vision for the organization
1.1.6  Communicate the vision
```

#### L4 Activities (selected high-value)
```
1.1.1.1  Monitor macro-economic trends
1.1.1.2  Analyze regulatory and political environment
1.1.1.3  Assess technology disruption patterns
1.1.1.4  Evaluate social/demographic shifts
1.1.2.1  Conduct competitive benchmarking
1.1.2.2  Map competitive positioning
1.1.2.3  Identify white-space opportunities
1.1.3.1  Identify key stakeholder groups
1.1.3.2  Conduct stakeholder interviews/surveys
1.1.3.3  Synthesize stakeholder insight into needs map
1.1.4.1  Evaluate core competency inventory
1.1.4.2  Assess resource strengths and constraints
1.1.5.1  Draft and stress-test vision scenarios
1.1.5.2  Validate vision with leadership team
1.1.5.3  Ratify vision statement
1.1.6.1  Build internal vision communication plan
1.1.6.2  Cascade vision to all organizational levels
```

#### L5 Tasks (sample)
```
1.1.1.1.1  Subscribe to economic indicator feeds
1.1.1.1.2  Summarize quarterly macro trends for strategy team
1.1.1.3.1  Identify tier-1 and tier-2 competitor landscape
1.1.5.1.1  Facilitate scenario planning workshop
1.1.5.1.2  Document 3 strategic futures (pessimistic/base/optimistic)
```

#### KPIs
- Vision awareness rate (% employees who can articulate vision)
- Vision-strategy alignment score (% strategic initiatives traceable to vision)
- Competitor coverage breadth (# competitors systematically monitored)

#### Integration Triggers
- Output → 1.2 (vision anchors strategy)
- Output → 7.7 (vision communicated through HR channels)
- Receives ← 3.1.1 (market research informs vision)
- Receives ← 13.7 (performance data informs vision refresh)

---

### 1.2 Develop Business Strategy

**Purpose:** Translate the vision into a concrete, time-bound plan for how the
organization will win — choices about where to play, how to win, and what not to do.

#### L3 Processes
```
1.2.1  Develop overall mission statement
1.2.2  Evaluate strategic options and define strategic direction
1.2.3  Formulate business unit strategies
1.2.4  Develop global business strategies
1.2.5  Coordinate and align functional and process strategies
1.2.6  Create and manage organizational transformation
1.2.7  Translate strategy into measures and targets
```

#### L4 Activities
```
1.2.2.1  Generate strategic options (growth, defend, diversify, exit)
1.2.2.2  Score options against criteria (risk, return, capabilities)
1.2.2.3  Select primary strategic direction
1.2.3.1  Cascade enterprise strategy to business units
1.2.3.2  Develop BU-specific strategic plans
1.2.5.1  Align marketing strategy (feeds 3.2)
1.2.5.2  Align product strategy (feeds 2.1)
1.2.5.3  Align HR strategy (feeds 7.1)
1.2.5.4  Align IT strategy (feeds 8.1)
1.2.5.5  Align financial strategy (feeds 9.1)
1.2.7.1  Build Balanced Scorecard or equivalent
1.2.7.2  Set annual targets per strategic theme
1.2.7.3  Publish strategic KPI dashboard
```

#### L5 Tasks (sample)
```
1.2.2.1.1  Facilitate strategic options brainstorm with executive team
1.2.2.2.1  Apply scoring matrix to each option
1.2.5.1.1  Translate revenue target into marketing demand goals
1.2.7.1.1  Map strategic objectives to measurable outcomes
1.2.7.2.1  Negotiate targets with business unit leaders
```

#### KPIs
- Strategic plan completion rate (% initiatives on track)
- Strategy-budget alignment (% budget tied to strategic priorities)
- Strategy cascade depth (% of org with role-level strategic objectives)
- Strategy refresh cycle time

#### Integration Triggers
- Output → ALL operating categories (budget allocation, priorities)
- Output → 9.1 (financial plan targets)
- Output → 7.1 (workforce planning)
- Output → 8.1 (IT roadmap alignment)
- Output → 13.4 (transformation initiatives)
- Receives ← 11.1 (risk assessment shapes strategy)
- Receives ← 12.2 (investor expectations shape targets)

### 1.3 Manage Strategic Initiatives

**Purpose:** Execute strategy through a managed portfolio of cross-functional
initiatives with clear owners, milestones, resources, and governance.

#### L3 Processes
```
1.3.1  Develop strategic initiative roadmap
1.3.2  Govern strategic initiative portfolio
1.3.3  Track and report initiative progress
1.3.4  Adjust strategy based on performance
```

#### L4 Activities
```
1.3.1.1  Identify and define strategic initiatives
1.3.1.2  Sequence initiatives by dependency and priority
1.3.1.3  Assign executive sponsorship
1.3.1.4  Allocate resources to initiatives
1.3.2.1  Establish initiative governance cadence
1.3.2.2  Review initiative health monthly/quarterly
1.3.2.3  Make go/pause/kill decisions on at-risk initiatives
1.3.3.1  Collect initiative status from owners
1.3.3.2  Produce consolidated initiative dashboard
1.3.3.3  Escalate blockers to executive team
1.3.4.1  Assess strategy-reality gaps quarterly
1.3.4.2  Revise strategy based on performance feedback
```

#### Integration Triggers
- Output → 13.2 (feeds program/project management)
- Output → 9.1 (initiative budgets feed financial plans)
- Receives ← 13.7 (performance data triggers strategy adjustment)
- Receives ← 11.1 (risk events may force initiative pivots)

---

### 1.4 Develop and Maintain Business Models

**Purpose:** Explicitly design and continuously validate how the organization creates,
delivers, and captures value — the logic connecting strategy to operations.

#### L3 Processes
```
1.4.1  Design the value proposition
1.4.2  Design the revenue model
1.4.3  Design operating model
1.4.4  Validate and iterate the business model
```

#### L4 Activities
```
1.4.1.1  Define customer segments and jobs-to-be-done
1.4.1.2  Articulate core value proposition per segment
1.4.2.1  Define pricing model
1.4.2.2  Define revenue streams
1.4.3.1  Map key activities, resources, and partners
1.4.3.2  Define cost structure
1.4.4.1  Test business model assumptions
1.4.4.2  Update model based on market signals
```

#### Integration Triggers
- Output → 2.1, 2.2 (product development aligns to value prop)
- Output → 3.2 (marketing strategy aligns to value prop)
- Output → 9.1 (revenue model anchors financial planning)
- Receives ← 3.1, 6.3 (customer insight validates/challenges model)
