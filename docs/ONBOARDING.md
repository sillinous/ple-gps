# Contributor Onboarding

Welcome to the Post-Labor Economics & Global Progress project. This guide gets you from "just arrived" to "actively contributing" as smoothly as possible.

---

## Step 1 — Orient Yourself (30 min)

Read these in order:

1. **[`README.md`](../README.md)** — what this project is and why it exists
2. **[`community/PLE_PRIMER.md`](PLE_PRIMER.md)** — the core Post-Labor Economics framework (David Shapiro's model and our extensions)
3. **[`community/ROADMAP.md`](ROADMAP.md)** — where the project is headed and what's actively being worked on
4. **[`CONTRIBUTING.md`](../CONTRIBUTING.md)** — how contributions work
5. **[`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)** — community standards

If you have questions after reading, **open a Discussion** rather than an issue — questions and exploratory conversations belong there.

---

## Step 2 — Find Your Domain

Look at the working groups in `CONTRIBUTING.md`. Which one aligns with your background or interests?

Don't overthink this — you can participate in multiple groups, and you can change focus. The goal is to find where your contributions will be most useful and most energizing.

**Not sure?** Start in **Community Infrastructure** or **Communications & Education** — both have immediate, concrete work that doesn't require deep prior knowledge of PLE theory.

---

## Step 3 — Introduce Yourself

Go to [GitHub Discussions → Introductions](../../discussions/categories/introductions) and post a brief intro:

- Who you are (as much or little as you want to share)
- What brought you here
- What you're hoping to contribute or learn
- Which working group(s) interest you

This is low-stakes and helps the community know who you are.

---

## Step 4 — Find a First Contribution

Look for issues labeled:

- `good first issue` — explicitly scoped for newcomers
- `help wanted` — active need, any experience level
- `discussion-needed` — needs perspective, not necessarily a PR

You can also browse **GitHub Discussions** and simply respond to threads where you have something useful to say. Quality engagement in discussions is a real contribution.

---

## Step 5 — Make Your First PR (when ready)

When you're ready to contribute something concrete:

1. Fork the repo
2. Create a branch (`docs/your-topic` or `fix/description`)
3. Make your changes
4. Open a PR using the template
5. Tag the relevant working group: `@ple-gps/theory`, `@ple-gps/technology`, etc.

Don't worry about it being perfect. The review process is collaborative, not evaluative.

---

## Working Group Meetings

Each active working group holds a recurring call (schedule in each group's pinned discussion). Attendance is open — show up, listen, and participate when you have something to add.

Meeting notes are committed to `docs/working-groups/[group-name]/meetings/`.

## Key Concepts to Know

Before diving deep, these concepts come up constantly:

| Concept | Short Definition | Where to Learn More |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Post-Labor Economics | Economic framework for a world where automation has displaced the majority of human labor | `community/PLE_PRIMER.md` |
| Universal Basic Income (UBI) | Unconditional cash transfers as a floor | `community/frameworks/UBI.md` |
| Post-scarcity | Conditions where material needs can be met without scarcity-driven competition | `community/frameworks/POST_SCARCITY.md` |
| Contributory economy | Non-wage forms of economic participation and value creation | `community/frameworks/CONTRIBUTORY.md` |
| Labor displacement curve | The projected timeline and pattern of automation's impact on employment | `community/research/DISPLACEMENT.md` |
